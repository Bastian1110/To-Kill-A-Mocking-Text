In recent times, there has been a surge in the popularity of Artificial Intelligence (AI) as a result of remarkable technological advancements and impressive experimental outcomes, particularly in the domain of image analysis and processing. Medical specialties like radiology, pathology, and oncology, which heavily rely on images, have embraced this trend and invested significant resources in research and development to harness the potential of AI for clinical applications. The adoption of AI as a mainstream tool in medical imaging tasks such as diagnosis, segmentation, and classification is growing, and ensuring its safe and effective use in clinical settings depends, in part, on well-informed healthcare professionals. This review aims to outline the fundamental technological foundations of AI, along with state-of-the-art machine learning techniques and their application in medical imaging. Furthermore, we explore emerging trends and future research directions in this field. This discussion aims to provide insight into how AI methods are becoming integrated into standard medical image analysis workflows, paving the way for the practical implementation of AI-driven solutions in clinical practice
