Medical image segmentation is a critical task within the realm of computer vision, playing a pivotal role in various applications such as diagnostics, treatment planning, and medical research. This study undertakes an extensive exploration of methodologies employed in medical research for achieving image segmentation, spanning from traditional techniques like thresholding, edge detection, region-based, and clustering approaches to modern artificial intelligence methods, notably deep learning techniques. The paper meticulously evaluates the strengths and limitations of each method, with a specific focus on analyzing different architectures utilized for medical image segmentation and assessing their performance. It aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of various segmentation methods, offering a comparative analysis of their effectiveness. Additionally, the document delves into recent technological advancements in segmentation, highlighting significant breakthroughs capable of revolutionizing the accuracy and efficiency of medical image analysis. Through an exhaustive compilation and detailed critique of results obtained from employing diverse segmentation strategies, the study presents multiple approaches' outcomes, along with an in-depth analysis of the strengths and weaknesses inherent in the techniques applied to medical image segmentation. This research contributes to enhancing the understanding of how these methods can be effectively applied within the medical domain, particularly in the realm of computer vision.
